Shared Mobility Equity Analysis - Providence, RI

Developed web app to perform various analytics on the scooter share datasets. Current display shows median trips per day broken down by census tract.

Overview

I worked on a student research team lead by Professor Alice Paul to to analyze the equity of the distribution and accessibility of scooter share services in Providence (RI). The outcome of this work was used to inform city policy decisions regarding the regulation of the private scooter rental operators in the city.

My Contributions

Census Data Identification and Manipulation

I was in charge of identifying relevant Census demographics information, acquiring the data, and manipulating it into a state usable for analysis. I referenced the data.census.gov web portal to select relevant census tables including population numbers, race, ethnicity, income, education, and language data for Providence, RI from the ACS 2018 5-Year Survey, I pulled these tables into R to use the Tidyverse package to combine the data into a central demographics dataset with standardized naming patterns. This code is viewable here. As a big believer in good documentation, I wrote a simple markdown file that provides more context to this stage of the project.
